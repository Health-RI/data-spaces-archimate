      • Goal
        • Empower individuals to access, control and share their electronic health data for the healthcare delivery
      • Outcome
        • Patients electronic health record available for treating health care providers
        • Each healthcare provider retains full control over the protection of personal data in their electronic health records
        • Patients can store their personal health record in a personal data vault
      • Requirement
        • A health care provider shall share information about the medical background and history of a patient
        • The patient has given consent to share her medical history
        • The data product must be submitted in a format where its shape constraints are both resolvable and machine-readable
        • The delivery of messages must be reliable
        • The referral must be electronically signed by the general practitioner
        • The transport channel must be encrypted
        • Data exchange must be compliant with HealthData@EU for cross-border communication.
        • Data exchange must ensure messages are delivered reliably, even if the receiver is temporarily offline.
        • Data exchange must ensure that a message is delivered and processed at least once, and often "at-most-once" or "at-least-once" delivery guarantees are required.
        • Data exchange must ensure that duplicate messages are identified and discarded to prevent unintended side effects.
        • Data exchange must ensure that message payloads are encrypted.
        • Data exchange must ensure that the sender is authenticated in a manner that ensures non-repudiation.
        • Data exchange should have a way to handle messages that fail to be processed after multiple attempts, moving them to a separate "dead-letter queue" for later inspection.
      • Principle
        • An electronic health record is a federated view of patient data
        • Health care providers provide access to electronic health records
        • Separation of Data and Transport
        • The data ensures non-repudiation, guaranteeing traceability to its origin
        • Data should remain at its source; questions should be brought to the data whenever possible
